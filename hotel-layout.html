Algonquin Hotel $ per night

Considered a living legend when it comes to hotels, the Algonquin is famously known for the Round Table. It has hosted some of the most famous literary and theatrical notables, some of them reported to have never left the hotel at all.

New York Marriot Marquis, New York CityMore Info
New York Marriot Marquis $ per night

Set in the Theater District and a block from Times Square, this modern, 49-story hotel is topped by a revolving restaurant. It's a 4-minute walk from the 42nd Street/Times Square subway station.

Straightforward rooms (some with views of Times Square) feature floor-to-ceiling windows, flat-screen TVs, WiFi (fee) and coffeemakers. Some rooms have pull-out sofas, and suites add living rooms. Select rooms give access to a club lounge with free breakfast and snacks.

Amenities include a stylish revolving restaurant, a casual eatery and a Starbucks cafe. There's also a business center, 55 meeting rooms, and fitness facilities. WiFi is free in public areas.

The Standard High Line, New York CityMore Info
The Standard High Line $ per night

A sleek 18-story tower rising above the High Line urban park (a former elevated train track), this hotel has floor-to-ceiling windows to take in sweeping Hudson River and city views. Within 4 blocks are Meatpacking District nightspots, Chelsea Market and the subway.

Room decor is minimalist and modern; rooms have free WiFi, flat-screen TVs, minibars and peekaboo rain showers.

For dining, there's a grill, new American bistro, bar, outdoor German beer garden and rooftop creperie (a disco at night), plus a rooftop supper lounge and brunch spot; the ground floor cafe has a winter ice rink and art installations. Guests can also borrow bicycles.
